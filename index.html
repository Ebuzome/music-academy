Our Story

Preserving Nigeria's Musical Heritage

Founded in 2023, Harmony Music Academy emerged from a passion to preserve and elevate Nigeria's rich musical traditions while embracing contemporary global music practices. Our journey began in a small Lagos studio with just two instructors and a dream to create a world-class music education institution.

Today, we stand as a beacon of musical excellence in West Africa, with state-of-the-art facilities in three major cities. Our mission is to cultivate the next generation of Nigerian musical talent by providing comprehensive training that honors our cultural roots while preparing students for global success.

At Harmony, we believe music is not just an art form but a powerful tool for personal transformation and cultural expression. Our unique pedagogy combines rigorous technical training with creative exploration, encouraging students to develop their authentic musical voice.

We're committed to creating an inclusive environment where students from all backgrounds can discover and develop their musical potential. Our graduates have gone on to perform with international orchestras, win prestigious awards, and establish successful music careers across Africa and beyond.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our programs and enrollment process.

What is the minimum age for enrollment?

We offer programs for all ages! Our youngest students start at age 4 in our Music Explorers program. For children under 4, we recommend our parent-child music classes. Instrument-specific programs typically begin at age 6 or 7, depending on physical development and instrument size.

Do I need prior musical experience?

Not at all! We welcome complete beginners. Our programs are designed to accommodate all skill levels. During your initial consultation, we'll assess your experience and recommend the appropriate starting point. Many of our most successful students began with no prior musical training.

What instruments do you offer lessons for?

We offer instruction for a wide range of instruments including piano, guitar (acoustic, electric, bass), violin, cello, flute, trumpet, saxophone, drums, and traditional African instruments like the talking drum, shekere, and kalimba. If you don't see your instrument listed, please contact us as we may still be able to accommodate you.

Are payment plans available?

Yes, we offer flexible payment options to make music education accessible. Most programs can be paid in monthly installments. We also offer sibling discounts and scholarships for exceptionally talented students with financial need. Contact our admissions office to discuss the best payment solution for your situation.

Can I schedule a trial lesson?

Absolutely! We encourage all prospective students to schedule a complimentary 30-minute trial lesson. This allows you to meet the instructor, experience our teaching approach, and ensure it's the right fit before committing. Contact us to schedule your trial lesson at any of our campus locations.

Do you host student performances?

Yes, performance is an integral part of music education. We host quarterly recitals at our performance hall, community outreach concerts, and an annual gala featuring our most advanced students. All students are encouraged but not required to participate in performance opportunities when they feel ready.

Do you offer certification?

Upon completion of our comprehensive programs, students receive a Harmony Academy certificate. We also prepare students for international certification through ABRSM (Associated Board of the Royal Schools of Music) and Trinity College London examinations. Our certification programs are recognized worldwide.

Are online classes available?

Yes, we offer both in-person and online instruction. Our virtual classes are conducted via secure video conference with the same qualified instructors. While we believe in-person instruction offers certain advantages, our online programs have proven highly effective, especially for students outside our campus cities.
